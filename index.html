About Me

I'm a generalist. An early interest in science and its history gave me an appreciation for cross-disciplinary perspectives. It was pursuing larger trends and seeking alien experience that I decided to move to Taiwan to learn Chinese. I try to approach the classroom with the same cautious spirit, hoping to be sensitive to the needs of my students to help them learn better ways of expressing themselves.

My focus has been aimed at some of the marvels of our era, chiefly perhaps cinema, globalization, and the web.

In 2004 I moved to New York for a year to study filmmaking in a happy program that involved making films on a weekly basis. I've been fortunate that since then, every couple of years, I've had friends who welcomed my help with their filmmaking projects.

By way of my film festival fanaticism, I came across Asian films that piqued my curiosity about the geopolitical rise of Asia. So I moved to Taiwan eight years ago. I studied Chinese my first year here and obtained a bachelor's degree in politics 2 after four more years. Now I'm writing a thesis to earn my MBA degree3.
